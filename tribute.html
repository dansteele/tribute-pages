Nikolai Kapustin

The iconic Russian jazz composer

Kapustin studied piano with Avrelian Rubakh and subsequently with Alexander Goldenweiser at the Moscow Conservatory. Kapustin acquired a reputation as a jazz pianist, arranger and composer in the 1950's.

Almost all his compositions are for piano. However, he has written 20 piano sonatas, six piano concerti, and other compositions for ensembles such as five saxophones and an orchestra (Op. 22) and a concerto for double-bass (Op. 76).

Kapustin's style is a fusion of classical virtuoso pianism and improvisational jazz.

    Here's a timeline of Kapustin's life

  • 1937 - Kapustin was born in Horlivka, Ukrainian SSR.
  • 1951 - At the age of 14, Kapustin moved to Moscow and started lessons with Avrelian Rubakh and later Alexander Goldenweiser.
  • 1957 - Kapustin's first opus, Op. 1: Concertino for piano and orchestra, was written.
  • 1969 - Kapustin was married to Alla Baranovskaya.
  • 1984 - Op. 40: Eight Concert Etudes in Jazz Style, my favourite opus, was composed.
  • 2016 - Op. 161: The Moon Rainbow, his last opus, was composed.
  • 1961 - Kapustin graduated from the Moscow Conservatory and became a member of the Oleg Lundstrem Big Band.
  • 1971 The first child of Kapustin, Anton Kapustin was born and later grew up to become a theoretical physicist in the US.
  • 1977 - Kapustin joined the Symphonic Orchestra of Cinematography to record music for the cinema until 1984.
